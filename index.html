What I can do for you

Most of my experience comes in working for startups. I have had to develop a very diverse skill-set while working for these companies. This has led me to spotting and executing on opportunities which would have otherwise been passed over. What I bring to the table, is my understanding of the big picture at a high level, and my intuition for knowing where potential business-impacting opportunities lay.

I am not just a web developer.
I am a consultant for web presence.

Business-First

Your website’s purpose is to support your business. At the heart of this, is the experience that your users have while visiting (UX). First and foremost, visitors must get what they came for, and must have as seamless an experience as possible.

This is the heart of my development philosophy.

I do not spend development hours trying to shave off 30 milliseconds of page load time. Instead, I prioritize tasks that directly impact the experience of your users.

Communication

Communication is at the heart of any business arrangement: one party communicates to the other what goals they expect to achieve.

Programming is a very a deep field, and programmers tend to immerse themselves in that world. This makes it easy to forget that most people do not. Communication has a massive impact on how smoothly business goes. This makes it one of the core tenets of my practice.

Weekly Progress Reporting

I track my time very closely and take note of specifically where my development hours are spent. I summarize this and other information in a weekly report. I try to be as non-technical as possible, in order to allow you to retain control of the development process.

Philosophies

  • Responsive

    Your website will be made with a mobile-first mentality. The clients of a typical B2C business will access the company website from a mobile phone more than 50% of the time. This makes mobile optimization critical for a presence on the web.

  • Dynamic

    If necessary, your website will be able to get data from a database and display it in the webpage. Think sites like Amazon, which has a products page, or Facebook which has a profile page. All users have their own versions of these pages, which come from the same template. Data is simply dynamically added in from a database.

  • Modular Design

    Code will be structured in such a way that components can plug into each other like lego. This makes the code easier to understand, and easier to scale.

  • Grandfathered Rates

    The very nature of web design suggests there will be maintenance. Appropriately, I expect to have a long term relationship with my clients. For all projects, I charge a single hourly rate throughout.

  • Search Engine Optimization (SEO)

    In the world of Google, how we design our websites has an impact on SEO (Search Engine Optimization). We are better off if we design our websites with the search engine in mind. If we want to get in Google's good graces, we'd better take its implications seriously during the design phase.

  • Advice on Technical Decisions

    I take an interest in all my client's work. As such, I am always on the lookout for tech-related opportunities. Throughout the development process, I will communicate technological opportunities that may present themselves.
